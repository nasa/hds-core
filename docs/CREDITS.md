# Credits

HDS Core exists because of the work of many people across NASA, the federal government, and the open-source community.

## HDS Core

HDS Core was originally developed within NASA's Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO) in 2026, with assistance from agency-approved AI tools such as ChatGSFC and NMC AI Hub.

The HDS Core Proposal, which defined the standardization and technical mapping of the HDS visual specification to the USWDS framework, was co-authored by a NASA designer (@courtbee) and a contractor developer (@marktimemedia) in 2024.

## Horizon Design System

The Horizon Design System visual specification (the comprehensive set of brand standards, component guidelines, and design patterns that HDS Core implements) was created by [Blink UX](https://blinkux.com/) under contract to NASA, 2021–2022. All HDS deliverables are fully NASA-owned.

## USWDS and NASAWDS

HDS Core is built on the strong shoulders of the [U.S. Web Design System (USWDS)](https://designsystem.digital.gov/), maintained by the General Services Administration (GSA) since 2015 and released into the public domain.

We've been inspired by many other federal and state agencies who have built their own design systems on top of USWDS, including the [Veteran Affairs Design System (VADS)](https://design.va.gov/), [New York State Design System (NYSDS)](https://designsystem.ny.gov/), and [Maryland Web Design System (MDWDS)](https://designsystem.maryland.gov/).

Before the Horizon Design System was even conceived, there was the trailblazing [NASA Web Design System (NASAWDS)](https://github.com/nasa/nasawds-old), a NASA fork of USWDS developed by @bruffridge and other GRC colleagues in 2016. We're proud to carry that legacy forward.
